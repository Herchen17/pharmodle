#1 — Metformin
drug id easy Endocrine
What drug is being described?
Clinical Context: This oral medication is the recommended initial pharmacotherapy for a common metabolic condition affecting over one million internationals, typically initiated alongside lifestyle modifications at diagnosis.
Key Feature: Unlike most other agents for this condition, this drug does not stimulate insulin secretion and carries negligible risk of hypoglycaemia when used alone. It is also associated with modest weight loss rather than weight gain.
Pharmacology Clue: Its primary mechanism involves reducing hepatic glucose output by activating AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK). It also improves peripheral insulin sensitivity in skeletal muscle.
Narrowing Detail: A rare but serious adverse effect is lactic acidosis, particularly in patients with renal impairment, sepsis, or conditions causing tissue hypoxia. It must be withheld before iodinated contrast procedures.
Identifying Detail: This biguanide was derived from the French lilac plant (Galega officinalis) and is typically initiated at 500 mg once daily with food, titrated to a maximum of 1 g twice daily. GI side effects are minimised with the extended-release formulation.

#39 — Tacrolimus
drug id hard Immunology
What drug is being described?
Clinical Context: This immunosuppressant is the backbone of modern solid organ transplant rejection prophylaxis, used in kidney, liver, heart, and lung transplantation. It is also used for severe atopic dermatitis (topical formulation) and refractory autoimmune conditions.
Key Feature: It inhibits calcineurin, a phosphatase enzyme required for T-cell activation, by forming a complex with an intracellular immunophilin. This blocks the nuclear translocation of nuclear factor of activated T-cells (NFAT) and suppresses interleukin-2 transcription.
Pharmacology Clue: It is primarily metabolised by CYP3A4 and is a substrate of P-glycoprotein, leading to numerous drug interactions. It has a narrow therapeutic index requiring regular therapeutic drug monitoring of whole-blood trough concentrations, with targets varying by organ transplanted and time post-transplant.
Narrowing Detail: Key adverse effects include nephrotoxicity (both acute and chronic), new-onset diabetes mellitus after transplantation (NODAT), neurotoxicity (tremor, headache, posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome), hyperkalaemia, and hypomagnesaemia. Alopecia distinguishes it from its older counterpart in the same class.
Identifying Detail: Originally isolated from the soil fungus Streptomyces tsukubaensis in Tsukuba, Japan, this macrolide lactone binds to FK-binding protein 12 (FKBP-12) — distinguishing it from the other calcineurin inhibitor which binds cyclophilin. It is approximately 100 times more potent than its predecessor.

#40 — Imiquimod
drug id hard Dermatology
What drug is being described?
Clinical Context: This topical cream is used for several skin conditions including superficial basal cell carcinomas (when surgery is not appropriate), actinic (solar) keratoses, and external genital and perianal warts (condylomata acuminata).
Key Feature: Unlike other topical treatments for skin cancers that directly kill cells, this drug works by stimulating the patient's own immune system to attack abnormal cells. It has no direct antiviral or antitumour activity.
Pharmacology Clue: It is an immune response modifier that activates toll-like receptor 7 (TLR7) on dendritic cells and macrophages, stimulating production of interferon-alpha, tumour necrosis factor, and other pro-inflammatory cytokines that mount a localised immune response against the target lesion.
Narrowing Detail: Application causes a characteristic local inflammatory reaction — erythema, erosion, crusting, and sometimes flu-like symptoms. This reaction is expected and indicates the immune response is working. Patients should be counselled that the treated area will look worse before it looks better.
Identifying Detail: Applied as a 5% cream three times per week (for genital warts) or five times per week (for superficial BCC) for several weeks. It must be washed off after 6–10 hours. It is not suitable for internal warts, invasive BCC, or immunosuppressed patients. The expected inflammatory response distinguishes it from simple topical treatments.
